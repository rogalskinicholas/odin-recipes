Chilaquiles Rojos

Description

Chilaquiles is a traditional Mexican comfort food, primarily served at breakfast, that centers on corn tortillas. The tortillas are cut into triangles and lightly fried until crisp, then simmered in either a vibrant salsa roja (red tomato and chili sauce) or a tangy salsa verde (green tomatillo sauce). The goal is to cook them just long enough for the chips to soften slightly while still retaining a bit of their structural integrity.

To finish the dish, it is traditionally topped with crumbled queso fresco, sliced raw onions or radishes, and fresh cilantro. It is frequently made heartier by adding a fried or scrambled egg on top. Because of its savory, spicy nature and satisfying texture, it is widely beloved both as a family staple and my personal favorite dish.

Ingredients

  • 8oz tortilla chips
  • 2/3 cup chicken stock
  • 4 tbsp olive oil
  • 1/2 onion
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 cups crushed tomatoes
  • 6 Chipotles peppers in adobo sauce
  • 1/4 red onion
  • 1/2 cup queso fresco
  • 1-2 Eggs
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • (Optional) Cilantro
  • (Optional) Radishes

Steps

Red Sauce Process
  1. Heat up a skillet to medium-high heat with olive oil. Sauté onions and garlic for 2-3 minutes with salt and pepper.
  2. Put the onions and garlic into a blender along with crushed tomatoes, chipotle peppers in adobe sauce, and stock. Blend until smooth.
  3. Pour sauce back into the skillet and simmer over low-medium heat for 10 minutes.
Fried Egg Process
  1. Heat up a nonstick pan over medium-high heat with olive oil. Crack one or two eggs into the pan.
  2. Fry eggs for 3-4 minutes until nice and crispy.
  3. Season eggs with salt and pepper.
Chilaquiles Process
  1. Increase the heat of the red sauce and bring it to a boil.
  2. Once at a boil, toss in the tortilla chips little by little.
  3. Stir and mix around the sauce and tortilla chips, seasoning with salt and pepper to liking.
Toppings
  1. Gently place the fried eggs on top of the chilaquiles.
  2. Sprinkle queso fresco, red onion, and optionally cilantro and radishes.

Serve on a plate and enjoy!
