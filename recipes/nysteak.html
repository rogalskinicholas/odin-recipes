New York Strip Steak

Description

The New York Strip Steak is one of my favorite high-protein classics. It can be practically served with any side. Known for its bold, beefy flavor and firm, satisfying texture, the meat is tender while still offering a "steak-lover's" chew. This recipe will make your mouth juicy.

Ingredients

  • 2 New York steaks
  • 3 tbsp butter
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 4 cloves garlic
  • 2 sprigs rosemary
  • Salt
  • Pepper

Steps

  1. Lay out the two New york steaks onto a cutting board and season all sides with salt and pepper.
  2. Heat a cast iron skillet or stainless steel pan to high heat. Once hot/smoking, add olive oil and spread evenly across the pan.
  3. Place both steaks into the pan with the fat side down, rendering down the fat for about 2 minutes until a crust forms.
  4. Flip the steaks and cook each side for 3-4 minutes, with thicker cuts taking longer.
  5. Once the sides are cooked and have formed a crust, put the heat on low. Wait 2 minutes for the pan to cool. Once cooled, add butter, rosemary, and garlic to the pan.
  6. Butter base the steaks with all that butter and herbs with a spoon for a minute.
  7. Very important! Rest the steaks for 7 minutes so they absorb the juices.

Cut into slices and enjoy!
